# collapse-resolution-framework
Computational framework for studying layered post-selection dynamics, detector geometry, survival/capture filtering, and admissibility constraints within Born-aligned probabilistic systems.
Collapse / Resolution Framework (CRF)

The Collapse / Resolution Framework (CRF) is a computational framework for exploring post-selection dynamics in constrained probabilistic systems. CRF investigates how Born-aligned first selection behaves under structured internal competition, detector asymmetry, dwell-dependent survival dynamics, and stabilization constraints.

The framework models a layered realization pipeline consisting of:

Born-weighted first selection,
exposure dwell dynamics,
survival/capture competition,
stabilization coupling,
and final probabilistic resolution.

Simulation results show that substantial internal asymmetry and geometry-dependent competition can emerge while final outcome statistics remain strongly Born-consistent across the modeled regimes. The work focuses on robustness, admissibility behavior, and constraint-preserving dynamics rather than Born-rule violation or replacement of quantum mechanics.

CRF is presented as an experimental computational framework intended for exploration, simulation, and analysis of constrained realization architectures.
